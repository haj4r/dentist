Periodontics

Gum (periodontal) disease is an inflammatory disease caused by bacteria accumulation above and below the gum. Treatment varies depending on the progression of the disease, with root planing and gum surgery needed in uncontrolled cases. Uncontrolled gum disease has been associated with increased risk of diabetes, heart disease and stroke.

Signs of Gum Disease

1. Red, swollen or bleeding gums.
2. Bad breath.
3. Receding gums.
4. Pain or discomfort in teeth, gums or soft tissues of the mouth.
4. Loosening of teeth over time.
